A paragraph is a group of sentences that develops one main idea. It typically includes a topic sentence, supporting sentences, and a concluding sentence, all working together to present a coherent thought or argument. Paragraphs are essential for organizing written work and making it easier for the reader to understand. Key Elements of a Paragraph: Topic Sentence: Introduces the main idea of the paragraph, often found at the beginning. Supporting Sentences: Provide details, examples, evidence, or explanations related to the topic sentence. Concluding Sentence: Summarizes or offers a final thought on the main idea of the paragraph. Structure and Organization: Unity: All sentences in the paragraph should relate to and support the topic sentence. Coherence: Sentences should flow logically and smoothly from one to the next, creating a clear and understandable message.
